CircuMat

A blended learning toolbox that helps actors related to city regions to identify the local material and goods flows.



Open tool

Project

CircuMat is a blended learning toolbox that helps actors related to cities, city regions industrial parks to identify the local material and goods flows in order to assess in a simple way the possibility and potential for ‘circular’ actions such as closed-loop materials metabolism. CircuMat is a modified (forked) version of Rama-Scene EIT Raw Materials project. CircuMat focuses on NUTS2 level classification as opposed to Rama-Scene country level analysis tool.

Environmentally Extended Input-Output (EEIO)

EEIO is a linear economic and environmental accounting model which provides details on the relationships between economic actors (e.g. industries and final consumers) and the environmental pressures they exert as a result of their activities.

Data

The database behind the platform is EXIOBASE V3.3.sm (2011) in Industry Technology Assumption (ITA) and product-by-product format. 200 products including secondary raw materials, 281 regions (NUTS 2016) and 10 characterised impact indicators are available for selection to allow for complex multi-variate analysis.

Production and consumption views

You can analyse the environmental and economic impacts resulting from the production or the consumption of goods and services. The production view analyses the impacts of the production of specific product categories while the consumption view analyses throughout the entire supply-chain of a consumed product category.

Modelling

Create final consumption scenarios by applying sets of relative changes to final demand. The changes are applied in sequence and results are recalculated.

Assumptions

Regional employment numbers in NUTS 2 level in NACE sectors are used to disaggregate the economic outputs of the sectors in EXIOBASE. (In other words, the output of a sector is a linear function of the number of people working in that sector.) Regional income data, gross capital formation and employment data are used to disaggregate the final demand. National accounts are the base-lines for the intensities. Cross hauling is not allowed while the disaggregation into subnational values (in other words, if the region is exporter, it is not an importer; and vice versa.) Final IO table is left unbalanced. RAS algorithm can be implemented for re-balancing.
